Big Idea

Dallas ISD schools should privately address the needs of students that are impacted by poverty. In this lesson students will research the various ways corporations support communities in order to propose a solution to help Dallas ISD schools to address childhood poverty in their city.

Question 1

How do you quantify/ measure impact?

Question 2

How do you estimate impact?

Question 3

How do you plan for impact (strategy)?

Question 4

How do you monitor impact (to improve program)?

Question 5

How do you evaluate impact (to prove social value)?

Goal

Your goal is to create a program or initiative that can help schools privately address the needs of their students.

Audience

Your audience is school leaders, elected officials in the City of Dallas and executives at AT&T, JP Morgan, and Microsoft. Be prepared to present your proposal to these caring community members in your city.

Products

Survey

You will create and dissimenate a survey to discover the needs of students in your school as it pertains to food, clothing, and school supplies.

Data Analysis

You will compile the data in a graph of your choice to understand what resources students need most to thrive in school. You will then come up with a formula to predict the same trends in other populations.

Research Paper

You will research ways those needs have been addressed in other communities by socially responsible corporations.

Proposal (PPT, Video, KickStarter)

You will create a SlideShow and a short video to include in a KickStarter campaign. The video and slide show should summarize the data you collect, your solution for the problem, and the resources you need to implement your solution. Be prepared to present your proposal to school leadership, community leaders, and industry partners.
